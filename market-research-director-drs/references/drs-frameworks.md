# DRS Frameworks, Metrics & Benchmarks

> Domain knowledge for the Market Research Director. Benchmark figures are
> **approximate** and drawn from widely reported industry ranges — always label
> them as estimates and verify against a primary source before publishing.

## 1. Scheme archetypes

| Dimension | Options | Trade-offs |
|---|---|---|
| Return location | Return-to-retail vs dedicated return points / RVMs | Retail = convenience & density; dedicated = cleaner logistics, higher capex |
| Governance | Centralized single System Operator / PRO vs decentralized (multiple) | Centralized = economies of scale, easier data; decentralized = competition |
| Collection tech | Manual count vs Reverse Vending Machines (RVMs) | RVMs = accuracy & fraud control; manual = low capex, higher labor |
| Scope | Deposit-only vs integrated with EPR | Integrated aligns incentives across packaging value chain |
| Ownership of unredeemed deposits | Producers / system / state | Determines who is funded by "breakage" |

## 2. Headline metrics (define these in every report)

- **Return / redemption rate** = containers returned ÷ containers sold. The KPI.
- **Collection rate** = material collected for recycling ÷ placed on market (EU SUP target: 90% by 2029 for PET bottles).
- **Material recovery rate** = recyclable output ÷ collected (net of contamination).
- **Unredeemed deposits ("breakage")** = value of deposits never claimed → a funding source.
- **Contamination rate** = non-target / spoiled material in the stream.
- **Cost per container** = total system cost ÷ containers handled.
- **Handling fee** = per-unit payment to retailers/return points.

## 3. Benchmark ranges (approximate — verify)

| Market maturity | Typical return rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mature, high-performing (Germany, Nordics, Baltics) | ~90–98% | High deposit value, dense RVM network, long-established |
| Established, mid | ~70–90% | Varies with deposit level & convenience |
| New / launching | ramping 40–80% over 2–4 yrs | Ramp-up curve is normal; don't judge year 1 |
| No DRS (kerbside/informal only) | often <50% for beverage containers | Baseline for feasibility comparison |

**Key driver:** return rate rises with **deposit value** (elasticity) and with
**convenience/return-point density**. Model both when forecasting.

## 4. Analytical frameworks to apply

- **Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)**: containers placed on market by material (PET,
  glass, aluminium, HDPE) × deposit value × expected return rate → deposit flows,
  handling-fee pool, and recovered-material tonnage.
- **Feasibility model**: capex (RVMs, counting centres, logistics) + opex (handling
  fees, transport, admin) vs funding (unredeemed deposits + producer fees + material
  sales). Output: cost-per-container, break-even return rate.
- **Consumer behavior**: willingness-to-return, willingness-to-pay deposit,
  convenience sensitivity, segmentation by demographic/geography.
- **Policy impact assessment**: effect on litter, collection rate, recycling
  quality, informal-sector livelihoods, producer cost.
- **Stakeholder mapping**: power/interest grid across producers, retailers, PRO,
  municipalities, consumers, regulators, informal sector.

## 5. Regulatory context (global)

- **EU Single-Use Plastics (SUP) Directive**: 77% separate collection of plastic
  bottles by 2025, 90% by 2029; 25%/30% recycled content targets. Major DRS driver.
- **EU Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR)**: pushes DRS where collection
  targets are missed.
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)**: producers fund end-of-life; DRS is one
  instrument within EPR.
- **Regional/national schemes**: numerous US states ("bottle bills"), Canadian
  provinces, Australian state CDS, UK (rolling out), and emerging schemes across
  Asia, Latin America, Africa. Confirm current status per jurisdiction before citing.

## 6. Data sources to recommend

Primary: producer sales data, RVM/return-point logs, consumer surveys, retailer
interviews. Secondary: PRO annual reports, regulator statistics, Eurostat, industry
associations (e.g., Reloop, TOMRA reports), academic studies. Always triangulate.
