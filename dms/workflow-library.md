## WF1: Research Impact Assessment

Purpose:

Understand the environmental, economic, and social case for DRS in the target geography.

Inputs:

- Geography context
- Waste and litter data
- Tourism or public space data
- Beverage/container consumption estimates
- International DRS benchmarks
- Local regulatory context
- Past pilot or collection data

Activities:

- Study comparable DRS systems.
- Estimate local environmental impact.
- Estimate public space or tourism relevance.
- Build economic and social case.
- Identify behavior barriers.
- Convert evidence into usable proof points.

Outputs:

- Research impact brief
- Case studies
- Business case
- Behavior insight notes
- Proof-point bank

KPIs:

- Research brief completed
- Number of validated proof points
- Stakeholder questions answered by evidence
- Evidence gaps identified
- Narrative inputs delivered on time

RACI:

- Responsible: Marketing
- Accountable: Marketing Head
- Consulted: Operations, Government Affairs, Sustainability/ESG, Data Team
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Research synthesis agent
- Benchmarking agent
- Evidence-to-message agent

Templates:

- Research impact brief
- Case study template
- Proof-point bank

Review frequency:

- Weekly before narrative approval
- Monthly after launch

Optimization triggers:

- Stakeholders ask questions evidence cannot answer.
- Public objections emerge around impact or credibility.
- Collection data contradicts assumptions.

## WF2: Stakeholder Mapping

Purpose:

Identify stakeholders who can accelerate, enable, influence, or block DRS adoption.

Inputs:

- Geography brief
- Regulatory context
- Retailer and brand landscape
- Municipality and pollution board context
- Media and influencer landscape
- NGO, school, and academic ecosystem

Activities:

- Map government and regulatory stakeholders.
- Map retailers, brands, municipalities, NGOs, journalists, academics, and influencers.
- Score influence and stance.
- Identify champions and blockers.
- Define engagement priority.

Outputs:

- Stakeholder map
- Influence matrix
- Champion list
- Blocker/risk list
- Engagement priority plan

KPIs:

- Stakeholder map completion
- Priority stakeholders identified
- Champion list completeness
- Stakeholders engaged
- Stakeholder readiness score

RACI:

- Responsible: Government Affairs
- Accountable: Government Affairs Head
- Consulted: Marketing, Partnerships, Operations, Sales/Enterprise where relevant
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Stakeholder mapping assistant
- Influence scoring assistant
- Briefing note agent

Templates:

- Stakeholder map
- Influence matrix
- Champion list

Review frequency:

- Weekly before launch
- Monthly after launch

Optimization triggers:

- A priority stakeholder is unengaged.
- A blocker gains influence.
- Coalition gaps delay launch.

## WF3: Narrative Development

Purpose:

Create the core DRS story and stakeholder-specific messages for the geography.

Inputs:

- Research impact brief
- Proof-point bank
- Stakeholder map
- Behavior barriers
- Local language needs
- Public sentiment signals

Activities:

- Build narrative house.
- Draft persona-specific messaging.
- Create FAQ kit.
- Develop whitepapers, opinion articles, roundtable themes, and explainers.
- Validate narrative with priority stakeholders.
- Translate and localize messages.

Outputs:

- Narrative house
- Persona messaging matrix
- FAQ kit
- Whitepaper or opinion article
- Roundtable brief
- Localized message bank

KPIs:

- Narrative approved
- Priority stakeholder message fit
- FAQ completeness
- Content assets completed
- Message recall or comprehension in testing

RACI:

- Responsible: Marketing
- Accountable: Marketing Head
- Consulted: Government Affairs, Communications, Operations, Legal/Policy where relevant
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Narrative strategist agent
- Persona messaging agent
- FAQ generator
- Localization assistant

Templates:

- Narrative house
- Persona messaging matrix
- FAQ kit

Review frequency:

- Weekly before launch
- Monthly after launch

Optimization triggers:

- Stakeholders interpret DRS inconsistently.
- Consumer questions reveal message confusion.
- Media coverage frames DRS incorrectly.

## WF4: Stakeholder Alignment

Purpose:

Prepare influential stakeholders to support, explain, and participate in the DRS launch.

Inputs:

- Stakeholder map
- Champion list
- Narrative house
- FAQ kit
- Demonstration plan
- Pilot or site visit options

Activities:

- Conduct stakeholder workshops.
- Share FAQ kits and briefing notes.
- Host demonstration center visits.
- Arrange pilot visits where possible.
- Capture concerns and update message assets.
- Secure visible champions and participation commitments.

Outputs:

- Workshop completion report
- Stakeholder briefing kits
- Demonstration visit log
- Commitment tracker
- Updated concern log

KPIs:

- Priority stakeholders briefed
- Stakeholder readiness score
- Champion commitments secured
- Open concerns resolved
- Retailer or partner participation commitments

RACI:

- Responsible: Government Affairs
- Accountable: Government Affairs Head
- Consulted: Marketing, Operations, Partnerships, Communications
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Briefing kit assistant
- Concern summarization agent
- Commitment tracker assistant

Templates:

- Stakeholder briefing kit
- Workshop agenda
- Concern and commitment tracker

Review frequency:

- Weekly before launch

Optimization triggers:

- Stakeholder readiness remains low.
- Repeated concerns emerge.
- A launch-critical stakeholder is not aligned.

## WF5: Consumer Awareness

Purpose:

Build public understanding and participation intent before and during launch.

Inputs:

- Narrative house
- Consumer behavior barriers
- Language requirements
- Launch locations
- Retail and machine locations
- School, airport, transit, social, and influencer channels

Activities:

- Run school awareness programs.
- Activate retail awareness materials.
- Activate airport or transit communication where relevant.
- Publish social and influencer content.
- Use local language explainers.
- Promote how, where, and why to return containers.

Outputs:

- Consumer awareness plan
- Channel calendar
- Retail/store assets
- School activation kit
- Social media content
- Influencer briefs

KPIs:

- Awareness reach
- Message comprehension
- Retail asset deployment
- School or community sessions completed
- Social engagement
- Search or inquiry volume
- Participation intent

RACI:

- Responsible: Marketing
- Accountable: Marketing Head
- Consulted: Retail/Partner Teams, Operations, Communications, Influencer Partners
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Consumer copy agent
- Local language adaptation agent
- Social content agent
- Channel planning assistant

Templates:

- Consumer awareness plan
- Retail asset checklist
- School activation kit
- Influencer brief

Review frequency:

- Weekly before and during launch

Optimization triggers:

- Awareness reach is low.
- Consumers do not understand deposit, return, or reward mechanics.
- Retail teams report repeated questions.

## WF6: Launch Readiness

Purpose:

Synchronize communication, stakeholder readiness, retail readiness, machine demonstrations, and launch-day execution.

Inputs:

- Launch date
- Awareness plan
- Stakeholder readiness status
- Retail onboarding status
- Machine deployment or demonstration status
- Media plan
- Event plan

Activities:

- Run T-90 awareness readiness.
- Run T-60 retail onboarding readiness.
- Run T-30 mass media readiness.
- Run T-15 machine demonstration readiness.
- Prepare launch-day event plan.
- Conduct go/no-go review.

Outputs:

- Launch readiness tracker
- T-minus launch plan
- Go/no-go decision
- Launch-day runbook
- Issue log

KPIs:

- T-minus milestones completed
- Retail onboarding readiness
- Machine/demo readiness
- Awareness asset deployment
- Launch issue closure rate
- Launch go/no-go confidence score

RACI:

- Responsible: DMS Pod Lead
- Accountable: DMS Pod Lead
- Consulted: Marketing, Operations, Retail/Partner Teams, Communications, Government Affairs
- Informed: Director Marketing, Leadership

AI agents used:

- Launch checklist assistant
- Issue log summarizer
- Runbook generator

Templates:

- Launch readiness tracker
- Launch-day runbook
- Go/no-go checklist

Review frequency:

- Weekly from T-90 to T-30
- Twice weekly from T-30 to launch

Optimization triggers:

- Any T-minus milestone is delayed.
- Communication readiness exceeds operational readiness.
- Launch risks remain unresolved at T-15.

## WF7: Reputation Management

Purpose:

Protect trust before and after launch by monitoring narratives, responding quickly, and amplifying credible proof.

Inputs:

- Media monitoring
- Social listening
- Stakeholder concerns
- Retail feedback
- Collection milestones
- Incident reports

Activities:

- Monitor media and public sentiment.
- Identify misinformation or negative narratives.
- Prepare rapid responses.
- Capture retail testimonials.
- Communicate collection milestones.
- Route operational issues to owners.

Outputs:

- Media monitoring report
- Rapid response brief
- Issue response log
- Retail testimonial bank
- Milestone communication

KPIs:

- Response turnaround time
- Negative issue closure rate
- Sentiment trend
- Misinformation incidents resolved
- Testimonials collected
- Milestones communicated

RACI:

- Responsible: Communications
- Accountable: Communications Head
- Consulted: Marketing, Operations, Government Affairs, Legal/Policy where relevant
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Media monitoring summarizer
- Rapid response drafting agent
- Sentiment classifier

Templates:

- Rapid response brief
- Media monitoring log
- Testimonial capture template

Review frequency:

- Daily during launch window
- Weekly post-launch

Optimization triggers:

- Negative sentiment rises.
- Misinformation spreads.
- Operational issues create reputation risk.

## WF8: Collection Optimization

Purpose:

Improve return behavior, retail participation, and collection efficiency using operational and behavioral data.

Inputs:

- Containers returned
- Return rate
- Cost per container
- Active users
- Retail participation
- Location-level performance
- Consumer feedback
- Retailer feedback

Activities:

- Analyze return patterns.
- Identify low-performing locations.
- Identify consumer friction points.
- Recommend behavior nudges.
- Update retail or machine communication.
- Feed insights into awareness and reputation workflows.

Outputs:

- Collection dashboard
- Location performance report
- Friction log
- Optimization recommendations
- Updated awareness messages

KPIs:

- Containers returned
- Return rate
- Cost per container
- Active users
- Retail participation
- Repeat returns
- Location performance improvement

RACI:

- Responsible: Operations
- Accountable: Operations Head
- Consulted: Marketing, Retail/Partner Teams, Data Team
- Informed: Director Marketing, DMS Pod Lead

AI agents used:

- Collection data analyst
- Friction classifier
- Optimization recommendation assistant

Templates:

- Collection dashboard
- Friction log
- Location optimization brief

Review frequency:

- Weekly after launch

Optimization triggers:

- Return rate is below target.
- Cost per container rises.
- Specific locations underperform.
- Consumer friction repeats.

## WF9: Narrative Amplification

Purpose:

Turn milestones and proof into stronger adoption stories for the current geography and future geographies.

Inputs:

- Collection milestones
- Impact data
- Stakeholder testimonials
- Retail testimonials
- Media wins
- Before/after visuals
- Community stories

Activities:

- Build milestone stories.
- Publish case studies.
- Create PR and social amplification.
- Equip stakeholders with proof.
- Update research and narrative assets.
- Package learnings for the next geography.

Outputs:

- Milestone campaign
- Case study
- PR story
- Social proof assets
- Updated playbook
- Next-geography briefing

KPIs:

- Milestones amplified
- Earned media mentions
- Stakeholder shares or endorsements
- Case studies completed
- Next-geography assets updated
- Positive sentiment movement

RACI:

- Responsible: Marketing
- Accountable: Marketing Head
- Consulted: Communications, Operations, Government Affairs, Retail/Partner Teams
- Informed: Director Marketing, Leadership

AI agents used:

- Case study writer
- PR angle generator
- Social proof asset assistant

Templates:

- Milestone story template
- Case study template
- Next-geography briefing

Review frequency:

- Monthly after launch

Optimization triggers:

- Milestones are achieved but not communicated.
- Future geography teams lack proof.
- Stakeholder confidence needs reinforcement.
