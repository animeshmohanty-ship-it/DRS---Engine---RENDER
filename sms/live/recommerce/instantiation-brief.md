## 2. Solution Thesis

What problem does this solution solve?

Enterprises have retired IT assets that lose value, create storage/security issues, and require responsible disposition. Recommerce gives them a structured way to recover value and show responsible circularity.

Why is the problem urgent now?

Asset refresh cycles, remote/hybrid work hardware churn, ESG reporting pressure, and procurement cost control make retired IT assets a recurring business issue.

Why should enterprises trust Recykal?

Recykal can position itself as a circularity partner that combines process discipline, buyer network access, documentation, and sustainability outcome reporting.

What is the measurable business outcome?

Recovered value from retired assets, reduced storage burden, faster liquidation cycle, and improved procurement/finance visibility.

What is the measurable sustainability outcome?

Assets reused or responsibly routed, reduced landfill risk, and circularity/ESG reporting evidence.

## 3. ICP

Company size: Mid-market and large enterprises with recurring IT asset refresh or large one-time liquidation need.

Sectors: IT/ITES, BFSI, education, healthcare, manufacturing, large corporate offices.

Obligation level: Medium; may vary by e-waste compliance and internal ESG policies.

Sustainability maturity: Medium to high; ideal buyers care about ESG documentation as well as value recovery.

Buying trigger: Asset refresh, office consolidation, data center refresh, employee device replacement, ESG reporting cycle, storage cost, audit/disposal requirement.

Disqualifiers: Very small asset volume, unclear asset ownership, no decision owner, high data-security risk without mitigation path, unrealistic resale value expectation.

## 11. Risks And Open Questions

Top domain risks: Asset valuation, data security, documentation, and routing model must be credible.

Top demand risks: Campaign may attract low-volume accounts unless ICP and disqualifiers are enforced.

Top sales handoff risks: Sales may accept vague interest without asset volume, timeline, or decision owner.

Top proof gaps: Need examples of recovered value, reuse/resale outcomes, and documentation outputs.

Open decisions:

- What minimum asset volume qualifies?
- What documentation/certificates can Recykal provide?
- Who owns valuation?
- What buyer/refurbishment partner network is available?
- What data wiping/security assurance is in scope?
