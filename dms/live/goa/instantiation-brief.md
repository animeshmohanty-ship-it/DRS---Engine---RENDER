## 2. Geography Thesis

Why is DRS important in this geography?

Goa is a high-visibility geography where beverage container leakage can affect tourism, public spaces, beaches, and civic reputation. A DRS launch in Goa can create a strong proof case for Recykal because the public problem is visible, stakeholders are easy to understand, and the narrative can connect clean tourism, responsible consumption, and measurable returns.

What visible public problem does DRS solve?

Littered beverage containers in tourist areas, beaches, retail corridors, transit points, and public spaces.

Why should government support it?

DRS can support cleaner public spaces, measurable recovery, tourism-sensitive sustainability, citizen participation, and a visible circular economy program.

Why should retailers support it?

Retailers can become convenient return points, improve customer participation, and show visible support for a cleaner Goa. Their support must be made operationally simple and reputationally positive.

Why should consumers participate?

Consumers should participate because returning containers becomes a simple civic action with a clear deposit/reward mechanism and visible environmental impact.

## 6. Narrative Direction

Core narrative: Return every bottle. Keep Goa clean. Make circularity visible.

Primary proof points: To be validated in Week 1 research impact brief; likely proof themes include container recovery, cleaner tourism zones, retailer participation, and measurable return milestones.

Local pride angle: Goa can become a visible model for clean tourism and citizen-led circularity.

Environmental angle: Returned containers reduce leakage into public spaces and improve material recovery.

Economic angle: Cleaner public spaces support tourism value and reduce unmanaged waste burden.

Consumer benefit: Consumers get a simple way to participate and recover deposit/reward value.

Retailer benefit: Retailers become visible partners in a cleaner Goa while helping customers participate.

Government benefit: Government gets a measurable public participation program linked to clean public spaces and circular economy outcomes.
