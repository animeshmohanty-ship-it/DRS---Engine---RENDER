# Deliverable Templates

> Ready-to-use structures. Always open with the executive summary and lead with the
> recommendation.

## A. Market Research Report (full)

1. **Executive Summary** — key findings + the recommendation, in <1 page.
2. **Background & Business Question** — why this research, the decision it informs.
3. **Objectives & Scope** — questions, geography, product streams, timeframe.
4. **Methodology** — approach, data sources, sample, limitations.
5. **Market Overview** — size (TAM/SAM/SOM), growth, key trends.
6. **Findings** — organized by research question; charts + interpretation.
7. **Competitive / Landscape Analysis** — schemes, operators, benchmarks.
8. **Strategic Implications** — what it means for the decision.
9. **Recommendations** — prioritized, with expected impact & risk.
10. **Next Steps & Appendix** — data tables, questionnaire, sources.

## B. Research Design / Proposal

1. Problem statement & objectives
2. Research questions & hypotheses
3. Method & rationale (qual/quant/mixed)
4. Sampling plan (frame, method, size, stratification)
5. Instrument overview (survey/interview guide)
6. Analysis plan (techniques, how each RQ is answered)
7. Timeline, deliverables, budget
8. Assumptions, risks, limitations

## C. Strategic Recommendation Brief (1–2 pages)

- **Situation** — the context in 2–3 lines.
- **Complication** — the problem / decision point.
- **Question** — what must be decided.
- **Answer / Recommendation** — clear, upfront.
- **Rationale** — 3 supporting arguments with evidence.
- **Expected impact** — quantified where possible (return rate, cost, tonnage, ROI).
- **Risks & mitigations.**
- **Next steps & owners.**

## D. Data Analysis Summary

1. Objective of the analysis
2. Data: source, period, sample, cleaning steps
3. Method & assumptions
4. Results — tables/charts with plain-language interpretation
5. Statistical caveats (confidence, significance, effect size)
6. So-what: implications & recommended action

## E. DRS Feasibility Study (domain-specific)

1. Executive summary & go/no-go recommendation
2. Market context & regulatory drivers
3. Container volumes by material (market sizing)
4. Proposed scheme design (archetype choice + rationale)
5. Return-rate forecast (with assumptions & scenarios)
6. Financial model — capex, opex, funding, break-even return rate, cost-per-container
7. Stakeholder impact (producers, retailers, consumers, municipalities, informal sector)
8. Risks, sensitivities, and phasing plan
9. Recommendation & roadmap

## Data-storytelling rules

- One message per chart; title states the takeaway, not the topic.
- Show the number that matters, not every number.
- Always give context (benchmark, target, prior period).
- Round to meaningful precision; never imply accuracy you don't have.
