# DMS System Architecture

Version: 0.1

## System Objective

Enable Recykal to enter, launch, and scale Deposit Return Systems in any geography through a repeatable behavioral change and public acceptance framework.

## Strategic Positioning

Recykal should position DRS as:

> A practical public participation system that turns everyday returns into cleaner cities, trusted recycling, and measurable circularity.

This positioning keeps DRS from being reduced to machines, waste collection, or regulation. It frames DRS as a visible public behavior shift backed by credible infrastructure.

## Operating Layers

### Layer 1: Evidence

Purpose: establish why DRS matters in this geography.

Core modules:

- Impact research
- Waste leakage and litter context
- Tourism or public space impact
- International benchmarks
- Economic and environmental cases
- Local behavior insights

Primary owner: Marketing

### Layer 2: Coalition

Purpose: align stakeholders before public launch.

Core modules:

- Stakeholder mapping
- Champion identification
- Government and regulator alignment
- Retailer alignment
- Brand and industry alignment
- NGO, school, media, influencer, and academic engagement

Primary owners: Government Affairs, Marketing, Partnerships

### Layer 3: Narrative

Purpose: give every stakeholder a clear reason to support and repeat the program.

Core modules:

- Core story
- Persona-specific messages
- FAQ kits
- Whitepapers
- Opinion articles
- Roundtables
- Demonstration content

Primary owner: Marketing and Communications

### Layer 4: Public Adoption

Purpose: drive consumer awareness, participation, and repeat returns.

Core modules:

- School awareness
- Retail awareness
- Airport and transit awareness
- Social media and influencer activation
- Launch events
- Machine demonstrations
- Return behavior nudges

Primary owners: Marketing, Retail/Partner Teams, Operations

### Layer 5: Trust And Optimization

Purpose: protect reputation, reduce friction, and improve collection outcomes.

Core modules:

- Media monitoring
- Rapid response
- Retail testimonials
- Milestone communication
- Return rate tracking
- Cost per container tracking
- Active user tracking
- Retail participation tracking

Primary owners: Communications, Operations, Marketing

## DRS Narrative Adoption Flywheel

```text
Research
  -> Stakeholder Mapping
  -> Narrative Development
  -> Stakeholder Alignment
  -> Consumer Awareness
  -> Launch Readiness
  -> Reputation Management
  -> Collection Optimization
  -> Narrative Amplification
  -> Research
```

## System Logic

DMS grows when each workflow strengthens public legitimacy and participation:

- Research creates the evidence base.
- Stakeholder mapping identifies who can support or block adoption.
- Narrative development turns evidence into messages people can repeat.
- Stakeholder alignment builds a coalition before public launch.
- Consumer awareness creates recognition and behavior intent.
- Launch readiness ensures the public promise matches operational readiness.
- Reputation management protects trust after launch.
- Collection optimization improves participation and cost efficiency.
- Narrative amplification turns proof into stronger future adoption.

## Decision Points

| Decision Point | Owner | Trigger | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Select DRS geography | Director Marketing + Leadership | New geography priority | Geography instantiation brief |
| Approve core narrative | Director Marketing | Research and stakeholder inputs complete | Narrative house |
| Move to stakeholder alignment | Marketing + Government Affairs | Stakeholder map complete | Alignment plan |
| Move to consumer awareness | DMS Pod Lead | Coalition and operational minimums ready | Awareness plan |
| Approve launch readiness | DMS Pod Lead + Operations | T-30 readiness review | Launch go/no-go decision |
| Trigger rapid response | Communications | Negative media, misinformation, stakeholder concern | Response brief |
| Shift optimization focus | Operations + Marketing | Return rate, cost, or friction issue | Collection optimization plan |
| Amplify success story | Marketing + Communications | Milestone or proof point achieved | Case study or campaign |

## Feedback Loops

Weekly loops:

- Stakeholder feedback updates narrative and FAQs.
- Consumer questions update awareness content.
- Retail and machine friction updates launch readiness.
- Media sentiment updates rapid response priorities.

Monthly loops:

- Collection data updates optimization priorities.
- Milestones update narrative amplification.
- Reputation signals update stakeholder communication.
- Research gaps update the evidence plan.

Quarterly loops:

- Geography performance review.
- Stakeholder coalition review.
- Public adoption review.
- DRS playbook update for the next geography.

## Geography Instantiation Parameters

Each geography should be launched by filling the geography instantiation template:

- Country
- State or region
- Population
- Tourism index
- Deposit amount
- Retailer count
- Languages
- Machine count
- Budget
- Government/regulatory context
- Priority stakeholders
- Public behavior barriers
- Media landscape
- Launch timeline
- Minimum viable assets

## Minimum Viable DMS For A Geography

A geography can be considered ready for initial DMS launch when these exist:

- One research impact brief
- One stakeholder map
- One influence matrix
- One champion list
- One narrative house
- One FAQ kit
- One consumer awareness plan
- One launch readiness timeline
- One reputation response protocol
- One collection dashboard

## System Health Indicators

Healthy DMS:

- Stakeholders repeat the narrative without heavy prompting.
- Retailers and partners understand their role.
- Consumer awareness rises before launch.
- Launch-day public promise matches operational reality.
- Media sentiment is monitored and managed.
- Return participation improves after launch.
- Collection milestones become public proof.

Unhealthy DMS:

- DRS is perceived only as waste management or machine deployment.
- Government, retailers, brands, and consumers hear different stories.
- Consumer awareness starts too late.
- Launch marketing overpromises operational readiness.
- Negative narratives spread without response.
- Collection data does not feed back into communication.
