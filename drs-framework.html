Phase 2 · "What are we going to do?"

Pre-planning — the Campaign Brief

Designed TAT · 17 Jul

The first human + AI phase. Its job is to write one artifact — the Campaign Brief (Charter) — that Planning must obey. It decides what, why, for whom, and within what limits — never how (that's downstream).

SWOT — the strategic snapshot AI synthesis

Auto-drawn from all of Market Research (Stages 2–6) and shown at the top of Pre-planning — the one-page read of where Recykal stands. The AI uses it to draft the brief's Situation & Challenge; you can edit it like any other section.

Strengths / Weaknesses

Recykal & Retearn's internal position — the assets and the gaps.

Opportunities / Threats

The external market forces working for and against the rollout.

The chat co-author — how the brief gets written

The Copilot stops being a read-only Q&A and becomes a co-author. Two artifacts stay in lock-step: the conversation and the living brief.

Propose → approve

The AI proposes a brief update ("set Objective = …"); you accept, tweak, or reject. It never silently puts words in your mouth.

Grounded in research

The chat always has the Phase 1 findings loaded, so it answers from your real stakeholder map — not generically.

Completeness nudging

It tracks which sections are still blank and pushes you until the brief is whole.

Two paths, one truth

Edit a section by hand or through chat — both write to the same brief. The brief is the single source of truth.

The flow of the phase
1
Entry

2
Draft AI

The AI Director drafts each brief section it can from the research.

3
Human You

You edit, override, and inject real-world inputs — mandates, hard dates, budget boundary, non-negotiables.

4
Challenge AI

The Director pressure-tests your calls — flags contradictions, vague objectives, priorities the research doesn't support.

5
Decide You

Confirm direction and lock the brief when it's whole.

6
Lock

The Campaign Brief becomes the source of truth for everything downstream.

Phases 3–5 · downstream (the goal)

Planning · Orchestration · Execution

Not yet designed

Where the campaign gets built, routed, approved, and run. The near-term milestone is a working Orchestration layer; execution can lean on the team's existing bots at first, but the ultimate goal is to build execution here too.

3
Planning TAT · 20 Jul

Turn the locked brief into a 360° campaign plan, broken into atomic, ownable tasks. This task shape is the contract with execution — it stays the same whether an external bot or a native module runs it.

4
Orchestration — the milestone TAT · 22 Jul

Each task gets an owner, an approver, and a capable executor (skill-based routing) → approve → dispatch → track in a control tower.

5
Execution — the goal TAT · later

Interim: route approved tasks to the team's existing bots (ad runner, WhatsApp, social). Ultimate goal: build execution natively in this bot.

Sequencing

Build the brain first (Research → Pre-planning → Planning). Get everything working through Orchestration. Only then decide native vs. separate execution bots.
