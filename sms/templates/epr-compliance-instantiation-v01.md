## 2. Solution Thesis

**What problem does this solution solve?**

Enterprises with EPR obligations (batteries, tyres, used oil, plastics) must prove they've collected and processed a mandated percentage of what they put into the market. Most don't have the collection infrastructure or documentation systems to prove compliance themselves. They need a partner who can handle collection, processing, and CPCB-accepted documentation.

**Why is the problem urgent now?**

- CPCB is intensifying enforcement. Penalties and show-cause notices are increasing.
- Board-level ESG reporting now requires documented compliance, not just good-faith effort.
- Many companies filed manually in year 1 and are now hitting the limit of what that approach can sustain at scale.
- Recykal's own investor communications (July 17 meeting) position EPR compliance as a proven revenue stream — this must become a structured engine.

**Why should enterprises trust Recykal?**

- CPCB-registered PRO (Producer Responsibility Organisation) — not a broker, a licensed compliance partner.
- Own collection network across geographies — supply chain is real, not subcontracted.
- USD 23M fundraise signals scale and financial stability (use in enterprise conversations).
- Existing clients are proof — reference cases should be harvested from current accounts.
- DRS work and Marketplace give Recykal credibility across the full material lifecycle, not just EPR certificates.

**What is the measurable business outcome?**

Zero penalties. Clean CPCB filing. Procurement and sustainability teams freed from compliance management. Enterprise ESG reporting with documented material traceability.

**What is the measurable sustainability outcome?**

Tonnes diverted from landfill or uncontrolled disposal. Battery/tyre/used oil material processed through authorised recyclers. Carbon avoidance from diverted material.

## 11. Risks and Open Questions

**Top domain risks:**
- Regulation changes faster than the playbook — Battery rules already amended once
- CPCB interpretation varies by state — compliance team must stay current
- Competitors (other PROs) are positioned similarly — differentiation must be Recykal's collection network + data traceability, not just a certificate

**Top demand risks:**
- 0% click rate blocks all conversion today — button CTA fix is the single highest-leverage action
- Association email list quality unknown — open rates are strong but are these the right ICPs?
- Single-threaded on Sustainability Heads — CFO and Procurement not yet in the conversation

**Top sales handoff risks:**
- No MQL/SQL definition means Yogendra's leads are not being formally handed to Sales
- No proposal template — Sales has to build from scratch every deal
- No reference contacts — enterprise buyers ask for references before signing

**Top proof gaps:**
- No documented case study from existing EPR compliance clients
- No quantified penalty data (the "cost of non-compliance" number that makes CFOs act)
- No compliance dashboard demo — buyers need to see what they're buying

**Open decisions:**
1. ACV target per EPR compliance deal — needed to set pipeline targets
2. Which Sales owner takes EPR qualified leads?
3. Is the MOFU offer a free workshop or a paid assessment?
4. Do we prioritise Battery (best open rate) or Used Oil (best open rate, niche) first?
